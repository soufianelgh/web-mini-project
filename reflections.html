Utilitarianism

To think of utility as supreme, to pursue and have interest in skills, occupations even hobbies for their mere effectiveness , stripping away the joy, not deliberately but because pursuing the mastery of said skills or hobbies is more relevant . Here ,knowing they can be balanced somehow doesn't prove as a solution. It happens I want to master drawing because it would come handy if one wants to draw a person's face only from their imagination (like a forensic artist) or to recreate a sence of a visited place , chess as means to sharpen pattern-recognition . language learning (although enjoyed as an activity ) the vast verbal prowess and the ability to read literature in original text, to allow for different ways of thought-formation, training so that one's standards in martial arts , calisthenics are at least more than enough. All this is always in mind to be attained .Anything really those were just examples. utilitarian in everything , almost like a trap of objectivity.

a place holder

I believe just like in chess where there are blunders , mistakes and then inaccuracies (after which the "unnecessary") , as humans we make those "inaccuracies" it's only natural to do, still objectively they are inaccuracies. Now they're not mistakes nor blunders so they don't seem to matter, only that just like in chess "inaccuracies" lead to mistakes which lead to grave or unpleasant outcomes and therefore in everyday life (wherein they seem to be everywhere), they aren't important until they actually are. To minimize or rather make inaccuracies you are aware of and is the goal, not optimization in the sense of perfection.
